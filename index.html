About

I started learning computer security at a young age with a focus on understanding how systems fail rather than how they are intended to work. My interests revolve around breaking assumptions made by developers, studying edge cases, and documenting recurring security mistakes.

Over time, this evolved into responsible security research, coordinated disclosure, and bug bounty work. I am particularly interested in authentication systems, trust boundaries, and backend logic vulnerabilities.
